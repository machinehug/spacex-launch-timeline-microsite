A rocket can be any type of engine, but related to space it is called a rocket engine. It does not need air, therefore it works in space. They either burn solid or liquid fuel, which turns the fuel into hot gas, which again pushes the rocket forward.

A spacecraft is a machine or vehicle designed to fly in outer space. They are used for communication, space and planetary exploration, Earth observation, navigation, and transportation of cargo and humans. Most spacecrafts need help getting into space, so they require a launch vehicle (carrier rocket).

The universe is s all of space and time, which consists of stars, matter, energy, planets and galaxies. The size of the universe is still unknown, but it is estimated to be 93 billion light years in diameter. Multiverse hypotheses argue that a universe is a part of a larger multiverse.

The Big Bang theory offers an explanation about how the universe began. It says it began about 13.8 billion years ago.

The beginning of Space Age started on October 4 1957 when the Soviet Union successfully launched the 1st artificial satellite Sputnik. It caught the attention of everyone and inspired space technology.

The Soviet Union had their 2nd successful artificial satellite launch with the spacecraft Sputnik 2 on November 3 1957. They wanted to see if any passengers could survive a launch into orbit, so the stray dog Laika was aboard. However, the first animals to be sent into space were fruitflies aboard a U.S. rocket on February 20 1947. They were sent to see the effects of radiation at high altitudes. Laika was the first living animal to orbit Earth. She died only a few hours after the launch due to overheating.

The United States had their 1st successful artificial satellite launch on January 31 1958, with the Explorer I.

Falcon 1 was SpaceX’s 1st spacecraft to successfully launch into space. This happened on March 25 2006. It was the first privately funded liquid-propellant rocket to reach orbit.

NASA (National Aeronautics and Space Administration) was created as a response to the Soviet Union's launch of Sputnik. NASA was created to oversee U.S. space exploration and aeronautics research.

NASA is counting on cooperation with private companies, and they have partnered with many companies, including SpaceX, one of their major contractors.

Space careers include aerospace engineer, avionics technician, scientist, aircraft mechanic and system assembler.
